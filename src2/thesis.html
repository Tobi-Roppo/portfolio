IoT-Based Rice Pest Insect Detector

Bachelor's Thesis

Efficient IoT-Based Rice Insect Pest Detector using YOLOv5 and Raspberry Pi

Rice, a critical global crop, faces constant threats from insect pests, leading to substantial yield losses and economic impact. Pest control strategies rely on precise timing and monitoring, with artificial light traps proving valuable. Pesticide spraying, though common, demands caution during early plant growth due to toxicity. Accurate insect identification is vital for effective control. The study introduces an innovative stationary pest infestation monitoring device using Convolutional Neural Network and Raspberry Pi, achieving 78.4% accuracy but highlighting the need for further refinement.

We developed an IoT-based system using a Raspberry Pi and a Pi cam to detect and classify rice adult-flying stage insects. The platform was designed with a focus on real-time monitoring, and the researchers used Roboflow for data labeling, data pre-processing, and data augmentation. We chose the YOLOv5 architecture for insect detection and classification, which involved training the model on a dataset of approximately 1140 labeled images. The platform successfully detected and classified insects in real-time and saved the results in JSON format. A user-friendly desktop app was created to display the data. Testing showed an impressive accuracy rate of 94.3%, with user and producer accuracy metrics, demonstrating the model's effectiveness. Furthermore, the study compared its results with a previous research effort by Cubillas et al. (2020), showcasing superior performance.

Training Results

Overall, Chapter 4 highlighted the successful implementation and effectiveness of the developed system in insect monitoring for rice fields.
